FAQ ‚Äî Design Rationale

Why Opus Trauma on DLBC instead of ART?
ART reduces room modes and decay ‚Äî the exact phenomena that make bass feel massive. On ART, subs output phase-inverted cancellation. On DLBC, 5 subs produce 100% forward-facing bass; +20 dB shelf stacks on room gain.
Why Night has +6 dB bed layer but -10 dB subs?
Subs: kill structural transmission (walls/floors). Bed: Fletcher-Munson ‚Äî at low volume, human hearing loses bass sensitivity. +6 dB restores perceptually natural balance.
Why G4 supported by G1 (not G5)?
Amplifier topology. G4 and G1 share the Outlaw 5000x. G5 is Denon internal. Cross-amp gain mismatch degrades ART coordination; same-amp support = clean.
Why no manual alignment for front Y-split subs?
Identical signal + symmetric placement + symmetric room (G1 L/R impulse overlap confirmed). Two subs sum as coherent wavefront.
Why Pre-out Only for the Outlaw channels?
Physically disconnects the A1H's internal power amps for those 5 channels. Eliminates back-EMF coupling to the preamp, frees PSU headroom for the remaining 10 Denon-driven speakers (especially during 6-channel Atmos peaks), and reduces thermal load for sustained demo-level sessions.
