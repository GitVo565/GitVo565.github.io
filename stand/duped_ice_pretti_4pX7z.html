Full Recap of the Incident

On January 24, 2026, around 9 a.m. local time, Alex Jeffrey Pretti, a 37-year-old U.S. citizen and ICU nurse, was fatally shot by two U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) agents—one Border Patrol agent firing a Glock 19 and one CBP officer firing a Glock 47—for a total of approximately 10 shots during an immigration enforcement operation in Minneapolis's Whittier neighborhood near 26th Street and Nicollet Avenue. The operation targeted an undocumented immigrant with a criminal history, but chaotic protests disrupted it, allowing the target to escape and injuring one agent. Bystander videos show Pretti filming agents with his phone while intervening to help a woman pushed to the ground during clashes; he was not holding a gun but had one holstered in his waistband—a high-end SIG Sauer P320 AXG Combat, a striker-fired 9mm pistol with a 4.6-inch threaded barrel (1/2x28 for suppressors), optic-ready slide (compatible with SIG Romeo optics), suppressor-height XRAY3 night sights, FDE anodized AXG alloy grip module, and equipped with extended 21-round magazines, a premium setup costing $1,500–$2,000, far from a basic or cheap carry gun. Agents pepper-sprayed him at close range, struck him, tackled him, and restrained him on the icy ground; one agent removed and secured his gun before shouts of "He's got a gun!" led to the shooting while Pretti was pinned amid a physical struggle with an armed suspect where the gun was perceived and the word “gun” was shouted. DHS initially claimed Pretti approached armed, "violently resisted" disarmament, and posed an imminent threat, justifying "defensive shots," with officials like Stephen Miller labeling him a "domestic terrorist" or "would-be assassin." However, a preliminary DHS report to Congress omitted any mention of Pretti brandishing or drawing the gun, noting only the shout and five-second delay before firing, and videos contradict key elements of the official narrative. Under settled self-defense law, officers are entitled to rely on fellow officers’ reasonable perceptions without personally confirming the threat; once a firearm appears during active resistance, the legal standard is reasonable perception of imminent deadly force, which some analyses argue was met here, as freeze-frame activism doesn’t override real-time dynamics, and the law does not require officers to wait to be shot—making this a tragic but potentially lawful use of force. Investigations by DHS, CBP's Office of Professional Responsibility, the DOJ's Civil Rights Division, and the FBI are ongoing, with bodycam footage under review but not released; the agents are on administrative leave. This was the second fatal shooting by federal agents in Minneapolis that month amid Trump's immigration crackdown, following Renee Good's death on January 7.

Background on Alex Jeffrey Pretti

Pretti was born November 9, 1988, in Park Ridge, Illinois, to parents Michael and Susan Pretti, with northern Italian ancestry. He grew up in Green Bay, Wisconsin, graduating from Preble High School in 2006, where he played football, baseball, ran track, participated in show choir, musicals, was a Boy Scout, and sang in the Green Bay Boy Choir. He attended the University of Minnesota, earning a bachelor's in biology, society, and environment in 2011, and obtained a nursing license in 2021. Pretti lived in Minneapolis's Lyndale neighborhood, was an avid outdoorsman who enjoyed hiking, mountain biking, and time with his dog, and had no criminal record beyond minor traffic tickets.
