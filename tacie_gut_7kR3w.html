The Running Gut

Strategic Intervention — The Osmotic Load

🔬 The Root Cause: "The Water Bomb"

Having to stop to poop on 100% of runs is a documented physiological clash in the Excalibur system known as The Osmotic Load. It is almost certainly a chemical issue, not a structural one.

Tacie's supplement stack acts as a mild, continuous laxative. Here's the chain reaction:

The Magnesium
She takes a massive dose of Magnesium (~250% of normal RDA) to manage muscle relaxation and QT history. Magnesium is an osmotic agent — it pulls water into her colon.
The Juice
Beet Juice and Tart Cherry Juice both contain natural sugars that increase gastric motility (speed of digestion).
The Trigger
She walks around all day with a highly hydrated colon. The moment she starts running, the mechanical vibration and the shunting of blood away from her gut instantly trigger the evacuation reflex to dump the water weight.

🛠 The Fix: "The Binder Protocol"

Execute the following Gut Defense tactics to stop mid-run bathroom breaks.

1The Magnesium Split (Mandatory)
She cannot take supplements (especially Magnesium or Vitamin C) anywhere near her run.
Action: Strictly enforce the 1 AM / 1 PM Split. Take 1 pill at breakfast, and the 2nd pill at 9:00 PM (right before bed). Taking them together or before practice floods the colon with water.
She must take the pills in the middle of a solid meal to slow down transit time.
2The "Gut Sponge" (Pre-Run Carbs)
To firm up stool during the week, add starch, not fiber.
Action: The 2:00 PM "Bridge" snack must be White Bread, White Rice, or Salted Pretzels. These lack fiber, clear the stomach quickly, and act as a "sponge" to absorb excess gastric juices and water in the gut.
The Ban: Zero fiber and zero fat within 2 hours of the run. No whole wheat, no nuts (allergic anyway), no apples with skin.
3Hydration Consistency
Action: She must sip water consistently between 2:00 PM and 5:00 PM. If she is dehydrated, the Magnesium wins the "war for water" in her body, making gut irritation and diarrhea much worse.
4The Emergency Override (Cut Osmotics)
Action: If you implement the Magnesium Split and the Starch Sponge and she still has to stop on runs, you must temporarily SCRUB the Beet Juice and Tart Cherry Juice. They may be causing too much osmotic pressure for her specific gut to handle during impact sports.
Quick Reference:
• Split Magnesium pills — 1 at breakfast, 1 at 9 PM (with food)
• 2 PM snack = white starch (pretzels, white bread, white rice)
• No fiber or fat within 2 hours of running
• Sip water steadily 2–5 PM
• If still failing → cut Beet Juice and Tart Cherry Juice
