‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Setup & Operations
Rear Switch Set to "INT" (Internal)
If set to "EXT", unit will run continuously and overheat.
Thermostat Mode Set to "AU" (Auto)
Cycles between High (Recovery) and Low (Maintenance).
SHUTDOWN PROCEDURE Always turn off via the Remote or Control Panel first. This keeps the fan running for ~60 seconds to cool the heating elements. Killing the breaker directly traps heat and creates a fire hazard/component failure.
üí° Tech Notes & Troubleshooting
  • The "Cold Air" Failure: The previous unit failed because the "Low" stage stopped producing heat. This is likely a burnt solder joint on the PCB relay or a severed wire at the heating element spade connector.
  • Repair Potential: The old unit is serviceable. A technician can often repair this by reflowing solder on the main relay or replacing the thermal cut-off fuse.
  • Maintenance Plan: The #1 killer of these units is drywall/sawdust intake.
    Action: Blow out the rear intake with compressed air annually (Next: April 2026).
